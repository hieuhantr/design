Venmo

Added a recurring payment feature for current users

Venmo (owned by PayPal) is a mobile payment service where users can transfer funds to each other within the U.S. Of the millenials who use mobile payments at all, more than 66% say Venmo is their go-to. When we analyzed why people use Venmo, we could see a pattern in paying for Food, Transportation, and Rent/Utilities.





From this data, we can see that “Food/Groceries” and “Rent/Utilities” (highlighted) are among the top three reasons to pay/request on Venmo. Millions of people are sharing rent, utilities, groceries bills on Venmo every day.

CURRENT PROBLEM

With how Venmo is set up, current users must go through the same task flow every time. Based on the payment recurrence, this same journey would be duplicated multiple times. However, this process doesn't ensure that users remember to pay on time, every time. Late payments result in frustration, cause financial penalties, and affect the relationship with their payees.

BUSINESS LANDSCAPE

Venmo’s direct competitors, Zelle and Cash App, both do not have recurring payment feature. In terms of annual payment volume, Venmo is the second after Zelle; however, Cash App is also growing rapidly. Developing this new feature will create a competitive advantage and increase Venmo’s payment volume for upcoming years.
